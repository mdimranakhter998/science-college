About Us

The Post Graduate Department of Statistics was established in 1948 and Prof. Laxmi Narayan was the founder member who started the teaching of Statistics at the Master’s degree level (M.A. and M.Sc.) and guidance to research scholars leading to Ph.D. in 1949. In 1966, with the initiative of the then HoD, Prof. Devendra Mishra the Population Research Centre (PRC) was established in the Department by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India with the Head of the Department of Statistics as its Chief/Director. Its purpose is to promote and undertake research work in the field of population studies. The Department and the PRC have their own libraries and each has a substantial collection of books and journals of Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Demography, Social Sciences and Family Welfare. Achievements The Institute of Correspondence Courses, now known as ‘Directorate of Distance Education’(DDE) was established in Patna University by the efforts of the then HOD (Statistics), Prof. Devendra Mishra in the year 1974 and became the first director of it. Two more teachers of this department, Prof. Amarendra Mishra (November, 2004-June 23, 2008) and Prof. R.S .Mishra (July 4,2015 - July 31,2015) also held this prestigious post of Director of DDE. Many eminent teachers of this department went on foreign assignments.
